Toyota of computers

June 13, 2022

Tags

  • Refurbish

The HP w/ AMD proc that’s been rocken for 5+ years was 9 years old. Worst of both worlds. Crappy AMD in a HP. Yuck. But it mostly ran and free is hard to beat. It’s been gradually degrading for some time now. Nail in the coffin was when DIMM slots starting going bad. It would only boot to OS if it first went into BIOS, did nothing, and was manually rebooted. Donezo.

Enter the ‘toyota of computers’. A Dell [my favvy] with pretty much any Intel chip…

Dell Optiplex 7010 MT Intel Core i7-3770 @ 3.40GHz 16GB RAM, 256GB SSD & 3TB HDD

…to be exact. 3rd Gen i7 is kinda old but again, you guessed it, free ninety nine. Upgraded from Windows 8 to Windows 10. A vast difference in performance despite both PCs being the same age. While swapping out drives from the old PC to the ‘new’ one, the SSD got upgraded aswell. Since the intermittent booting might be the SSD showing it’s age also, even though a glance at SMART results show nothing wrong.

At worst a $30 part, at best, there’s already an unused SSD lying around. It’s worth not having the potential headache, the machine is already open. Best to start new, when starting new. Even if it is with an old machine.
